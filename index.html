Operator’s Note

I am an independent crypto investor and trader. My work centers on understanding how blockchains can improve capital allocation, execution, and ownership structures over the long term.

I prefer clarity over complexity. I aim to allocate capital with high conviction in areas I understand deeply, while maintaining full transparency about both results and process.

This site exists as a simple record of where I stand and what I am focused on.

Track Record & Lessons

I have experienced both strong periods of growth and significant setbacks. In one cycle I reached a peak in net asset value, followed by a drawdown of approximately 98%. The recovery back to break-even came through focused, high-conviction decisions rather than broad exposure.

Along the way I also made several NFT trades. Some of these were driven more by narrative and enthusiasm than by durable fundamentals. They proved expensive but useful reminders about the difference between temporary excitement and lasting value.

These experiences reinforced several practical lessons: the importance of position sizing, the cost of emotional decisions, and the value of staying within one’s circle of competence. I intend to carry these lessons forward if I manage capital for others.

Operating Principles

Clarity over cleverness

I prefer straightforward structures and language. Complex instruments or narratives that require constant explanation usually signal unnecessary risk.

Skin in the game

Alignment matters. Any capital I manage will be alongside my own.

Long-term orientation

I am interested in protocols and markets that can compound in usefulness over many years, not quarters.

Transparency as default

Performance, process, and mistakes should be discussable. Hidden track records create hidden risks.

Current Areas of Focus

Real-time execution and scaling infrastructure

Interest in chains and systems designed for low-latency, high-throughput on-chain activity.

On-chain financial primitives

Perpetuals, options, and real-world assets. Focus on how these can be structured with better capital efficiency and clearer risk.

Internet Capital Markets and on-chain governance

Mechanisms for raising and allocating capital directly on-chain, including DAO structures and futarchy-style decision making.

On Managing External Capital

I am exploring the possibility of managing external capital on a discretionary basis through a modest-sized vehicle. The initial target scale is small. The priority is maintaining full discretion while operating with complete transparency.

I am open to both traditional structures and crypto-native approaches, provided they allow clear decision-making and honest reporting to participants.
